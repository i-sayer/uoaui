Thank you for taking students on attachment as a teaching practice for undergraduates (MBChB) for the Department of General Practice and Primary Health Care, University of Auckland.

Medical students’ general practice placements are:

  • Year 4: 2 weeks community hospital, A&M or rural general practice
  • Year 5: 4 weeks urban general practice
  • Year 6: 6 weeks rural general practice

Year 4 Students

Year 4 students are placed in either community hospital, rural general practice, or urban accident and medical clinics for a two week attachment. Students will be exposed to a variety of primary care experiences including opportunities to assist with procedural skills with nurses, and consultations with doctors. The opportunity to work with other health professionals such as pharmacy, podiatry, and physiotherapy are greatly encouraged. To offer you greater flexibility we now have staggered start dates for our year four students with a one week overlap. You are more than welcome to take overlapping students.

Payments are $500 per week per year 4 student.

Year 5 Students

In year 5 students spend 4 weeks on placement in an urban general practice.  This attachment provides an excellent opportunity to acquire skills and develop attitudes within the context of community-based settings. During their attachment students will attend a 1 day Hospice visit and a 1 day Symposium at Tamaki Campus.  We are often short of Practices for the first attachment of the year so your support at this time would be much appreciated.

Payments are $500 per week per year 5 student.

Year 6 Students

Our Year 6 students attend a rural regional practice for six week placement  giving them the opportunity to work within teams in a Rural setting.

Year 6 Students are required to engage in all Practice activity as a General Practitioner would.

We are often short of Practices for the first attachment of the year so your support at this time would be much appreciated.
